Project GreenChain - Autonomous Supply Chain Auditing Environment

OVERVIEW
Project GreenChain is an autonomous supply chain auditing environment built on the OpenEnv specification. It simulates a global procurement and logistics network where an AI agent acts as a "Sustainability Officer" tasked with minimizing carbon footprints without collapsing the corporate budget.

CORE ARCHITECTURE (OpenEnv Spec)
The environment follows a strict step(), reset(), and state() API, utilizing Pydantic models for type-safe interactions:

Observation Space: A dictionary containing current supplier lists (cost, carbon index, location), active shipments (lead time, perishability), and a "Greenwashing Risk" score for unstructured logistics data.

Action Space: Discrete and continuous actions allowing the agent to SwitchSupplier(old_id, new_id), RerouteShipment(shipment_id, route_hubs), or FlagForAudit(record_id).

Reward Function: A multi-objective scalar:
R = (Budget Efficiency × Sustainability Score) / Delivery Delay Penalty

TASK HIERARCHY & GRADERS

Level 1 (The Basic Swap):
Objective: Replace a Tier-1 supplier with a 40% lower carbon alternative.
Success Criteria: Carbon footprint reduces by >30% while maintaining a budget increase of <10%.

Level 2 (The Logistics Puzzle):
Objective: Optimize a 3-hub shipment route to minimize "transportation miles."
Success Criteria: Perishability of goods remains >0.8 while emissions drop by 15%.

Level 3 (The Deep Audit):
Objective: Identify three inconsistencies in a "Logistics Manifest" (mock OCR text) that indicate fraudulent carbon reporting.
Success Criteria: Precision/Recall on identified fraudulent records >0.9.
